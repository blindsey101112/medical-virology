Influenza Virus

Biology

The influenza virus is an RNA virus of the Orthomyxoviridae family.

The virus is enveloped, segmented, and negative-sense. It has eight RNA segments encoding 11 proteins, including haemagglutinin (HA) and neuraminidase (NA).

Epidemiology

Influenza is a global disease with seasonal epidemics.

Influenza A affects humans, birds, and other mammals, while Influenza B predominantly affects humans. Pandemic potential arises from antigenic shifts in Influenza A.
