Influenza Virus

Biology

The influenza virus is an RNA virus of the Orthomyxoviridae family.

The virus is enveloped, segmented, and negative-sense. It has eight RNA segments encoding 11 proteins, including haemagglutinin (HA) and neuraminidase (NA).

Epidemiology

Influenza is a global disease with seasonal epidemics.

Influenza A affects humans, birds, and other mammals, while Influenza B predominantly affects humans. Pandemic potential arises from antigenic shifts in Influenza A.

Clinical Features

Symptoms include fever, cough, sore throat, muscle aches, fatigue, and headache.

Severe cases can lead to pneumonia, respiratory failure, and death, particularly in the elderly and immunocompromised.

Diagnostics

Common diagnostics include Rapid Antigen Tests and PCR.

  • Rapid Antigen Detection Tests (RADTs): Fast but less sensitive.
  • Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR): Highly sensitive and specific, considered the gold standard.
  • Viral Culture: Rarely used due to time constraints.
  • Serology: Retrospective diagnosis and epidemiology.

Therapeutics

Treatment includes antivirals such as oseltamivir and baloxavir.

Early treatment reduces severity and duration. Antiviral resistance monitoring is crucial.

Infection Prevention and Control

Prevention includes annual vaccination and hand hygiene.

In healthcare settings, droplet and contact precautions are recommended. Respiratory etiquette helps reduce transmission.
