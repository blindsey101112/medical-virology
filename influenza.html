Influenza Virus

Overview

Influenza viruses are enveloped, segmented, negative-sense RNA viruses belonging to the Orthomyxoviridae family.

Structure

The virus has eight RNA segments and key proteins including haemagglutinin (HA) and neuraminidase (NA).

Clinical Features

Influenza typically causes fever, cough, sore throat, muscle aches, and fatigue. Severe cases can lead to pneumonia.

Prevention and Treatment

Annual vaccination is the primary preventive measure. Treatment includes antivirals such as oseltamivir and baloxavir.
