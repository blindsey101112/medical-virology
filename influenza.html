Influenza Virus

Biology

The influenza virus is an enveloped, segmented, negative-sense RNA virus belonging to the Orthomyxoviridae family. It has eight RNA segments encoding 11 proteins, including haemagglutinin (HA) and neuraminidase (NA).

Epidemiology

Influenza is a global disease with seasonal epidemics. Influenza A affects humans, birds, and other mammals, while Influenza B predominantly affects humans. Pandemic potential is associated with antigenic shift in Influenza A.

Clinical Features

Symptoms include fever, cough, sore throat, muscle aches, fatigue, and headache. Severe cases can lead to pneumonia, respiratory failure, and death, particularly in the elderly and immunocompromised.

Diagnostics

Diagnostic methods include:

  • **Rapid Antigen Detection Tests** (RADTs): Fast but less sensitive.
  • **Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR):** Highly sensitive and specific, considered the gold standard.
  • **Viral Culture:** Rarely used due to time constraints.
  • **Serology:** Used for retrospective diagnosis and epidemiological studies.

Therapeutics

Treatment includes antivirals such as neuraminidase inhibitors (e.g., oseltamivir) and polymerase inhibitors (e.g., baloxavir). Early treatment reduces severity and duration.

Infection Prevention and Control

Prevention includes annual vaccination, hand hygiene, and respiratory etiquette. In healthcare settings, droplet and contact precautions are recommended.
